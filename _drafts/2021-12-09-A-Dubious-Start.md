# A Dubious Start
Before even getting to the airport I have to drive all over to get tested for covid-19. Now I have two tests that say I'm fine, and they expire one after the other so I don't get stranded in Chicago's O'Hare airport. You would think traveling to another country during a pandemic would be, “easy”.
Regardless of all these caveats, I'm pretty excited to finally be travelling abroad, even though the first flight has me in a plane with just three rows of seats. The first stop on my journey is the windy city, Chicago, Illinois. Once I've navigated my way across the sprawling airport I'll be flying to London on a Boeing 787-9 (read, a big ol' plane). The final destination is the ever so beautiful, Nice, France.
As I'm writing this on my first flight, out my window is a sea of black and then a fantastically laid out grid of lights like some sort of glowing oriental carpet floating in dark waters. That is no carpet however, but Toronto, the city that invented the Tor network or something like that. On this flight I also have maximum leg room because the woman sitting next to me in the aisle seat just moved to another empty row, score!
